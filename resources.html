Study Tips

  • Review your notes within 24 hours of each lecture to move concepts into long-term memory.
  • Practice problems daily rather than cramming before exams - consistency beats intensity.
  • Work through examples step by step before attempting homework on your own.
  • Form a study group to discuss difficult concepts and quiz each other.
  • Use office hours - professors and TAs are there to help, and showing up demonstrates initiative.
  • Teach a concept to someone else; if you can explain it clearly, you truly understand it.
  • Break large assignments into smaller chunks and set mini-deadlines for each piece.
  • Don't just read the textbook - work the examples with a pencil and paper as you go.
  • Sleep matters. Your brain consolidates what you learned during rest, so pull fewer all-nighters.
  • When you get stuck on a problem, step away for 10 minutes. A fresh perspective often unlocks the answer.
