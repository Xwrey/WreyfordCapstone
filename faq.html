Frequently Asked Questions

General

What subjects do you tutor?

I tutor Calculus I, Calculus II, CS 1301 (Intro to Computer Science), and CS 1302 (Principles of Computer Science). These are the courses I've completed and feel confident teaching at a peer level.

Who are your sessions designed for?

My sessions are designed for college students - mostly at UWG, but I'm happy to work with anyone taking equivalent courses at other schools. Whether you're struggling to pass or aiming for an A, I can help.

What makes your tutoring different from other options?

I'm a peer tutor, which means I recently took the same classes you're in right now. I remember what was confusing, which professors test on what, and the shortcuts that actually work. It's not a generic lecture - it's a conversation about the stuff that's tripping you up.

Do I need to bring anything to a session?

Bring your textbook, class notes, any assignments you're working on, and a calculator if your course requires one. For CS sessions, have your laptop with your IDE set up. The more context I have about what you're studying, the more targeted the session will be.

Scheduling & Availability

How do I schedule a session?

Head to the Book page, pick a session type, and complete the purchase through PayPal. I'll email you within 24 hours to lock in a date and time that works for both of us.

What are your available hours?

I'm generally available Monday through Friday, 3:00 PM to 6:00 PM. During finals season or midterms, I sometimes extend those hours. If you need a time outside that window, just ask - I'll do my best to make it work.

How far in advance should I book?

I'd recommend booking at least 48 hours ahead so we can find a good time slot. That said, if you have an exam tomorrow, reach out anyway - I'll try to squeeze you in if I can.

Can I book recurring weekly sessions?

Absolutely. If you want consistent help throughout the semester, grab the 4-Session Bundle on the Book page. We'll set up a recurring slot that works with your schedule.

Pricing & Payment

How much do sessions cost?

A 30-minute session is $25 and a 60-minute session is $45. There's also a free 15-minute diagnostic if you're not sure what you need yet, and a 4-session bundle for $160 that saves you $20. Full details are on the Services page.

What payment methods do you accept?

I currently accept payments through PayPal. You can pay with a PayPal account, debit card, or credit card through their checkout process. Everything is handled securely through PayPal's platform.

Do you offer refunds?

If you cancel at least 24 hours before your session, I'm happy to reschedule or issue a full refund. Cancellations within 24 hours may be charged a partial fee since I've reserved that time slot for you.

Is the diagnostic session really free?

Yes, completely free. It's 15 minutes where I figure out where you stand and what kind of help would benefit you most. No payment info needed - just contact me to set it up.

Session Details

What does a typical session look like?

It depends on what you need. Some students come with specific homework problems; we work through those together. Others want a concept explained from scratch, so I'll walk through it with examples. For CS sessions, we might code together on your screen. There's no rigid format - I adapt to whatever helps you learn best.

Do you offer online sessions?

Yes. I offer both in-person sessions on the UWG campus and online sessions via Zoom. Online sessions work great - we can share screens, use a virtual whiteboard, and you get the same one-on-one attention.

Can I bring a friend to a session?

My sessions are one-on-one by default since that's where I can give the most focused help. If you and a friend want to study together, reach out and we can discuss a group rate or format that works.

What if I need to go over time?

If we're on a roll and close to a breakthrough, I won't cut you off mid-problem. A few extra minutes here and there is no big deal. If you consistently need longer sessions though, I'd recommend booking the 60-minute option.

Will you do my homework for me?

No, and you wouldn't want me to - you'd bomb the exam. I'll walk you through the approach, help you understand the concepts, and guide you through tough problems, but the work is yours. That's how you actually learn.

Subjects & Coursework

I'm in Calculus I and completely lost. Can you help?

That's literally the most common thing I hear. Calc I trips people up because it's a big jump from high school math. I'll help you build a solid foundation with limits and derivatives, and we'll work through problems until they start to click.

What topics do you cover in Calculus II?

Integration techniques (u-sub, integration by parts, trig sub, partial fractions), improper integrals, sequences, series, convergence tests, and Taylor/Maclaurin series. Basically, the whole course.

What programming language do you tutor for CS 1301?

Java or Python. We'll cover variables, control flow, functions, loops, strings, lists, file I/O, and basic OOP concepts - everything in the typical intro CS curriculum.

What does CS 1302 tutoring include?

CS 1302 goes deeper into object-oriented programming, data structures (linked lists, stacks, queues, trees), recursion, generics, and algorithmic thinking. We'll work through problems using Java and make sure you understand not just the "how" but the "why."

Can you help with courses you don't have listed?

It depends on the subject. If it's a closely related math or CS course, I might be able to help. Shoot me a message through the Contact page and we can figure it out.

Policies

What is your cancellation policy?

Cancel at least 24 hours before your session and there's no charge - we'll reschedule or refund you. Cancellations within 24 hours may be charged a partial fee since I turned down other students to hold your spot.

What if I no-show?

If you don't show up and don't let me know, the session is forfeited. Life happens, so just send me a quick text or email if something comes up and we'll work it out.

Do you have an academic integrity policy?

Yes. I follow UWG's academic integrity guidelines. I will never help you cheat on an exam, write code for a graded assignment, or do anything that violates your course's honor code. My job is to help you understand the material so you can do the work yourself.

What if I'm not satisfied with a session?

Let me know. Seriously. If a session didn't meet your expectations, I want to hear about it so I can adjust. I'm always looking to improve, and I'd rather fix the problem than lose a student.
